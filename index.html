Documentation Methodology

How I work.

Documentation is a product. Every project follows the same disciplined process — from discovery to a maintained, living document that stays accurate as the product evolves.

Step 01
🔍
Discovery & Scoping

Working directly with the product — and often building it — to understand what needs documenting, who the audience is, and what they need to accomplish. When I can't build it, I interview product owners and engineers to gather accurate technical context before writing a single word.

Step 02
🗂️
Information Architecture

Planning the content structure before drafting. Deciding content types (concept, task, reference), defining the topic hierarchy, identifying reuse opportunities, and structuring the KB or doc set so users can navigate by task, not by how the product was built.

Step 03
✍️
Draft, Review & Accuracy

Writing iteratively against established style standards (Microsoft Style Guide, Google Developer Documentation Style Guide for developer-facing content). Sending drafts to SMEs and product stakeholders for technical accuracy review before any content is published or handed off.

Step 04
🚀
Publish & Maintain

Publishing to the appropriate channel — help centre, GitHub Pages, PDF, or in-app — and maintaining documentation through product updates and feature releases. Documentation is not done at first publish. A doc that's wrong is worse than no doc.

Step 05
📊
Measure & Improve

Tracking real outcomes: support ticket reduction, self-service rates, stakeholder feedback, and user behaviour signals. The Bitrix24 KB reduced tier-1 support dependency by approximately 40%. Documentation that can't be measured can't be improved.
